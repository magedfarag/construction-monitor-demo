# Source Strategy

## Executive view

The uploaded inventory contains 48 candidate sources. The spreadsheet summary flags 18 as truly free/public and 34 as Middle East `Yes`, with additional rows marked `Partial` or `Depends`. The recommended free/public shortlist in the uploaded inventory already points to Sentinel-1/2, Landsat/HLS, Earth Search or Microsoft Planetary Computer, municipal open data where available, OSM-based geospatial services, AISStream, AISHub, Global Fishing Watch, and OpenSky.

This document converts that inventory into a delivery-oriented strategy. It answers four questions:

1. Which sources should ship in the MVP?
2. Which sources should wait until phase 2?
3. Which sources are valuable but should remain premium/future?
4. Which sources should be excluded for now?

## Strategy principles

- Prefer free/public before freemium before commercial.
- Prefer sources that explicitly support the Middle East.
- Prefer standards-based interfaces (STAC, OGC, REST, WebSocket) over opaque integrations.
- Penalize sources with high licensing ambiguity or strong operational dependency risk.
- Do not confuse public access with unrestricted commercial reuse.
- Do not commit to authority-specific construction feeds until a target authority is validated.

## Notes by source family

### Imagery
- **Phase 1 priority**: Copernicus Sentinel-1, Sentinel-2, Earth Search, and either CDSE directly or Planetary Computer where access convenience matters more than provider purity.
- **Why**: STAC interoperability, free/public access, and Middle East suitability.
- **Do not promise**: sub-meter site-level monitoring from public imagery.

### Maritime
- **Phase 1 priority**: AISStream only for bounded AOIs and pilot windows.
- **Phase 2**: AISHub or Global Fishing Watch as secondary validation feeds.
- **Premium future**: MarineTraffic, VesselFinder, Spire Maritime where commercial-grade history/coverage is required.

### Aviation
- **Phase 1 priority**: OpenSky only where its non-commercial posture fits the pilot.
- **Phase 2**: ADS-B Exchange for broader operational comparison.
- **Premium future**: FlightAware, Flightradar24, Spire Aviation.

### Administrative/public records
- Use only where the target authority exposes usable public datasets or ArcGIS services.
- Treat BLDS as a normalization aid, not as a live feed.
- Do not build deep Accela/OpenGov/Tyler integrations until a customer explicitly needs them.

### Basemap/geocoding
- Start with openrouteservice, Nominatim, and Overpass for AOI search and enrichment.
- Plan for self-hosting or a commercial fallback before scale.

## Strongest counterarguments

1. **"Public sources are too weak for real operational intelligence."**  
   Often true for small-site construction change or guaranteed live coverage. The architecture therefore preserves clean upgrade paths for premium imagery and telemetry.

2. **"Middle East authority data may simply not exist in reusable form."**  
   Also true in many cases. The system should treat public records as optional enrichment, not as the backbone.

3. **"Using too many providers early will slow the team down."**  
   Correct. That is why the recommendation is a narrow MVP shortlist, not all 49 rows at once.
